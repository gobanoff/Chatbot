Vilnius, the capital and largest city of Lithuania, is the most populous city in the Baltic region. Known for its well-preserved Old Town architecture, it is considered one of Europe's largest historic old towns. It was named a European Capital of Culture in 2009 and one of fDi's 25 Global Cities of the Future in 2021.

Geographically, Vilnius is located in southeastern Lithuania at the confluence of the Vilnia and Neris rivers, about 312 km from the Baltic Sea and the Lithuanian seaport Klaipėda. It spans an area of 402 km², with 29.1% occupied by buildings, 68.8% by green spaces, and 2.1% by water. The city also includes several lakes, notably Balžis, in its northern outskirts. Its cultural significance includes a number of museums and galleries.
